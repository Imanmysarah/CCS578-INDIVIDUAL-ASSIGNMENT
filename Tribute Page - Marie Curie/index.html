Marie Curie: A Pioneer of Science

Marie Curie, the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences (Physics and Chemistry).

Marie Curie (1867â€“1934) was a physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains the only person awarded Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

Born in Warsaw, Poland, Curie moved to Paris to study at the Sorbonne, where she met her husband and scientific partner, Pierre Curie. Together, they discovered the radioactive elements polonium and radium. Her groundbreaking work laid the foundation for modern physics and cancer therapies.

Despite numerous obstacles as a woman in science, Curie broke through barriers, inspiring generations to come. Her legacy continues to influence science and society today.
